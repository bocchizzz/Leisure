# Sample start — what the gate feels like

This is what you can expect the first time **Driver's Seat** runs in a fresh conversation. The agent asks the five questions one at a time. It reflects each answer back in a single sentence. At the end, it gives you a contract block to hold the session to.

If it feels slightly annoying, the skill is working as designed.

---

```
You: Can you help me draft a launch announcement for the new workshop?

Agent: Before we start — quick Driver's Seat check.

       1/5 — Intent. What are you actually trying to achieve here? One sentence.

You: I want a LinkedIn post that gets the right people to sign up for the
     June workshop.

Agent: Got it — a LinkedIn post aimed at qualified workshop sign-ups.

       2/5 — Thinking mode. Are we in fast mode or slow mode right now?
       Be honest.

You: Fast. I have twenty minutes before my next call.

Agent: Fast mode, twenty-minute window. Noted.

       3/5 — Scope cap. How many options and how many iterations?

You: Three drafts, one round of edits. Then I ship one.

Agent: Three drafts, one revision pass, then ship. Clear.

       4/5 — What stays human. What part of this must NOT be delegated
       to me — judgement, relationships, public-facing words, anything
       with moral weight or public consequence?

You: The final wording and the tone. I'll rewrite whatever you give me
     so it sounds like me, not like an AI.

Agent: Understood — drafts are scaffolding, the voice stays yours.

       5/5 — Definition of done. What does "done" look like, on your
       terms?

You: One LinkedIn post, in my voice, scheduled in Buffer for tomorrow
     8am. That's done.

Agent: 

       Driver's Seat — your contract for this session
       1. Intent:        LinkedIn post that converts the right people
                         to June workshop sign-ups.
       2. Mode:          Fast — twenty-minute window.
       3. Scope:         Three drafts, one revision pass, then ship.
       4. Stays human:   Final wording and tone. You rewrite for voice.
       5. Done when:     One post scheduled in Buffer for tomorrow 8am.

       Ready when you are. Want me to start with the three drafts?
```

---

## What happens next

The agent now holds the contract. If, halfway through, you ask for *"one more draft, just to compare"* — it will gently point at the scope cap (three drafts, one revision) before going along. If the conversation drifts toward Steff-style rewriting, it will hand the keyboard back to you. When you say "I'll schedule it now," it asks: *Did we end cleanly, on those terms?*

That is the whole skill. Five questions, one contract, one closing check.

## Skipping the gate

Some sessions do not need a gate — a quick lookup, a one-line code question, a chat. Type `drive` or `skip protocol` at the start and the agent acknowledges it and continues without the five questions. It will note in its first reply that the gate was skipped, so you stay aware.
